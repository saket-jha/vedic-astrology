Original Definition of Arudha Pada

BPHS (Brihat Parāshara Horā Shāstra) is very clear about the function of Arudha. According to BPHS, Arudha has two functions : its main role is about the MEANS through which the Bhāva gives its fruits, and its secondary role is that it helps or retards the magnitude of the fruit of its Bhāva depending on the conditions of the Arudha. For instance, if 11H in the Arudha of Lagna is malefic , then the source of income will be unethical : this is the CHIEF function of Arudha. But if the 11H of Arudha Lagna is strong, then the fruit of 11H of Lagna chart will be further strengthened in magnitude of income too. "Pada" in Sanskrit means "means" to attain some goal. That is why it denotes FOOT also, because foot or step is needed to reach somewhere. There, Pada in astrology means the characteristics of MEANS through which results of a bhāva are attained. This view is based on BPHS.

Ethical or unethical MEANS to fulfill one's objectives are related to Arudha and other PADAS. If 3H from AL is malefic, you will try to obtain the fruits of your D-1's 3H through malefic Parākrama and you will succeed in your malefic efforts if that house is strong. Twelve houses of Parākrama-pada show various details of QUALITY and QUANTITY of means and ends of your Parākrama. And Graha-pada of lord of 3H in D-1 will show the time of that fruit which will be obtained during the dashā of that planet. Other divisionals and their padas have similar properties.

All versions of BPHS (Brihat Parāshara Horā Shāstra) describe correct method of computing AL and other Pada charts, which I need not reproduce here. But two points are ignored by a majority of astrologers which need to mentioned here :-

AL or Arudha-Lagna and other Pada charts are based on Bhāva-chalita according to classics, because they are based on Bhāvas and their Lords.

The Degree of AL should be used to find out the beneficence or maleficence of AL on account of Vargas, but this view does not allow any change in computation of AL. For instance, if AL's navamsha is exalted, it add to the beneficence of AL. After all, verse-28 (verse-29 in English edition) in AL chapter of BPHA states that the yogas of AL are deduced along same principles which are used to deduce yogas of Kārakāmsha chart , and in latter case navāmsha plays an important role in evaluating D-1's Kārakāmsha.

Arudha Pada (AL) Ill-Defined as Perception : Truth versus Māyā

There is no classical support in any ancient text about Arudha being related to perceptions as some modern astrologers are propagating. "Arudha is about perceptions" is a modern invention. Mental perceptions are linked with not Lagna's pada charts but with Chandra Kundali and its pada charts. Lagna is not Mind but Physical existence. Moreover, perception is an ever-changing , subjective and vague term. When astrologers are fighting over basic OBJECTIVE facts, it is useless to argue about SUBJECTIVE perceptions.

There are countless persons who regard this world as Truth. But this view is not VEDIC. Vedic Astrology must not oppose the Vedic view. The last verse of Yajurveda is the foundation of Vedānta ("anta" of Veda) ; it explicitly states :

'This golden (=attractive) world of senses is the lid which hides Truth, and Truth is the Purusha which is same in me as in the Sun-God.'

Everyone deserves the right to propound his/her views, but not in the name of some ancient philosophy. Vedic Astrology cannot go against Vedic Thought.

Māyā versus Satya is also a modern invention, and this line of thinking is diametrically opposite to Vedic-Puranic philosophy which regards both This World as well as the Perceptions about This World as Māyā, Satya is the pure Atman.

How many astrologers use Pada and Upapada in actual chart readings ? If someone does not use these things, how he/she can gain any experience ?

Modern Definitions of Upapada

Upapada is defined variously by various modern interpretors. There is no traditional commentary on Hora-shāstra of any rishi. Even the basic texts have not been properly preserved. This lack of good commentaries has provided an opportunities to moderners to advance new theories without substantiation or logic.

BPHS defines Upapada as follows :

प्रधानस्य अङ्ग-भावस्य अनुचरस्य पदम् ही यत् । तदेव-उपपदम् गौडम् किम् उप-आरूढम्-उच्यते ॥

It means : "Anga (lagna) bhāva is pradhāna or chief, the pada of its 'anuchara' (follower) is called Upapada, Upa-aroodha or Gauda".

The term "anuchara" has been variously defined by moderners because there is no traditional definition of this term. Jaimini Sutra gives the following definition :

उपपदम् पदम् पित्रनुचरात् ॥ १ ॥ (प्रथम अध्याय, चतुर्थ पाद)

It means : "Upapadam follows the Aroodha-Pada as a son follows the father."

Hence, Jaimini Rishi does not differ from the definition of his guru Veda Vyāsa Ji's father Sage Parāshara. But why these rishis used vague term like "anuchara", instead of giving the definition in easier and clearer terms ?

Following the analogy of son-father, the editor of Chowkhamba edition of BPHS , Pt Devachandra Jha, wrote that upapada is first pada of 5H (5th house) from Lagna.

But the editor of Vārānasi's Sampoornānanda Sanskrit University's edition of Jaimini Sutra, Pt Kamlākānt Shukla, wrote that upa means near, hence upapada must be near the lagna, hence first pada of 2H or 12H from lagna must be upapada, depending on even or odd sign. There is a third school which regards first pada of 12H as upapada in both odd and even signs.

None of these commentators provided either any case studies or other evidences in favour of their views. Now, let us consider what was the original meaning of upapada according to Sages Parāshara and Jaimini.

Original Definition of Upapada

Significance of Upapada according to BPHS and Jaimini Sutra

An analysis of all the verses of BPHS and all sutras of JS (Jaimini Sutra) regarding upapada, we find that upapada's first house is exclusively concerned with various attributes of WIFE and offsprings from her. Is it not senseless to correlate wife with 5H (5th house) , 2H or 12H ? 2H has been related to second wife, but not with offsprings. But the chief function of upapada is first wife and not second wife. Regarding the significance of upapada according to BPHS and JS, it is not far-fetched to link upapada with the pada of 7H. But how 7H can be "upa" with respect to lagna ?

Upa means near, below, lower, etc. Is not 7H just below lagna in Parāshari-style chart (which terms 5H and 9H as Trikona, and they are actually triangular in shape, 1H-4H-7H -10H are called Kendras and are actually in centre in Parāshari-style chart) ? Hence, upa does not means 2H or 12H only, it may mean 7H also.

Similarly, anuchara has been misinterpreted to mean 5H, although 5H has no connection with wife which is the chief topic of upapada. The article Origin and Meaning of Horoscopic Houses (click here) explains the original ideas behind the twelve houses of horoscope, which proves that all twelve houses were related to opposite houses in cause - consequence relationship. This cause - consequence relationship can also be metaphorically described as "leader - anuchara" or "father - son " relation. Thus, in the light of original cause-consequence or leader - anuchara relationships between opposite houses like 1H and 7H. it is not far-fetched to define upapada as pada of 7H from lagna, more so because 7H is related to Kāma. Kāma has two aspects : wife and offsprings. The first or chief house of Upapada also has exactly these two topics : wife and offsprings. Some people may doubt the relation of 7H with offsprings, but they must consider the relation of Kāma as well as of wife's fortunes with number and conditions of offsprings. Following section shows how D7 is related to Kāma(7H) and D9 is related to Dharm-patni (needed for performance of Vedic Karmakāndas and Yagyas, not Kāma-patni of 7H).
